— history —

The minimalism art style made way in the 60s and 70s. It had been introduced in the 50s but had made its debut in homes and art museums in the 60s and 70s. This started to take over the abstract movement that had just taken over the years prior. As it took over homes, it became the new way to decorate your homes. At first it was more artwork, then that progressed into furniture, architecture, etc. People became tired of the clutterness and wanted to feel clean and minimal, hence the name minimalism.

Dan Flavin "monument" 1 for V. Tatlin 1964

— artists —

Carl Andre, Dan Flavin, Donald Judd, Sol LeWitt, Agnes Martin and Robert Morris were artists who inspired this movement. They became the artists to make this movement known in different aspects. There are a few different types of minimalism art. There is painting, scuptlures, architecture, design, etc. These artisits are great examples of the different art styles. The minimalism movement is known as the highest form of pure beauty, the simplicity and cleanliness of the art style bring calmness which draws people in.

Anges Martin, "White Flower" 1960

— more information —

Minimalism art is seen as truthful, it does not hide anything, it is not overwhelming you. It is exactly what it is, and does not pretend to be anything else. Today, minimalism is all about the simplisity of it all. A design where it is not clutered or overwhelming. It is meant to calm the mind of any distractions and to focus on what you are seeing right in front of you. Minimalism is not always grey and dull. Minimalism can be colorful yet simple, it walks the fine line between overwhelming and underwhelming.
